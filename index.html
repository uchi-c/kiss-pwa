⚠️
Do not do this

Do not use two condoms at once.

Do not reuse a condom.

Do not use oil-based lubricants with latex condoms.

Do not put a condom on halfway through sex.

Do not store condoms in hot places for long periods.

Do not use expired condoms.

🆘
If the condom breaks

Stay calm and stop right away.

Pregnancy risk: ask about emergency contraception as soon as possible (best within 72 hours, and up to 5 days depending on method).

HIV exposure risk: ask about PEP urgently (must start within 72 hours).

Arrange STI testing and follow-up with a clinic.

Go quickly to a clinic, pharmacy, or support service for personal advice.

Where can I buy Kiss Condoms? +
Tap any “Find a Stockist” button in Game Modes or open Find Support → Support Contacts for local stockist guidance.
What do I do if a condom breaks? +
Stop immediately, replace with a new condom, and consider emergency contraception and STI/HIV testing support as soon as possible.
Are condoms 100% effective? +
No. Correct and consistent use greatly improves protection, but no method is perfect.
How do I know the right condom size? +
Most users start with regular fit (53mm nominal width here). If condoms slip or feel too tight, seek guidance from trained providers for better fit options.
Can I use oil-based lubricants with condoms? +
No. Use water-based or silicone-based lubricants with latex condoms. Oils can weaken latex and increase breakage risk.
How should condoms be stored? +
Keep in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat. Avoid storing for long periods in wallets, cars, or tight pockets.
What if this is my first time using one? +
Practice opening and rolling onto clean fingers first. In real use, pinch the tip, roll fully down, and hold the base during withdrawal.
What if I have latex allergies or irritation? +
Stop use and seek medical advice. A provider can suggest non-latex alternatives and check whether irritation is from fragrance, lube, or another cause.
